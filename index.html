Center for Community Innovation (Research)

Over the summer of 2018 I worked for Center for Community Innovation under Professor Karen Chapple. I worked on Data Science projects like estimating the number of units under rent control in Berkeley as well as converting large text files (25gb+) to databases so that the lab could better understand gentrification in the greater New York Area.
Another project that I worked on was creating interactive maps to show the movement of all groups of people from all Bay Area counties to other locations. I also built a map that displays the change in demographics and rent throughout all neighborhoods in the Bay Area.

SafeWorld (Hackathon Project)

My team of 4 built this website during CalHacks 2017, this website navigates the user through the safest parts of San Francisco. We used a database of over 850,000 crime reports to find the safest path, our algorithm takes into account severity of crime as well as how recently it occured. Our VR app visualized the amount of crimes per block around SF through a 3D bar graph. Video
Our small team won 2 awards: one for best Virtual Reality application and the other for best travel application.
This was done over 36 sleepless hours, so this is a prototype.

Data Farm (Personal-Team Project)

Over the summer of 2018, some friends and I began to build a smart vineyard managment tool. The service leverages smart field sensors with an IoT connected irrigation system to manage the amount of water used by the farm in conjunction with the health of the grapes.
I helped set up the data infrastructure and built a web portal so that the farm staff can monitor the readings of the sensors. The next step in this project is to create statistical indicators to identify the health of the grapes. We also plan to add an algorithm to optimize water usage.

Cardinal Newman Alerts

This is an app I built for my high school, all programming done by me with the UI being designed by another student.
It allows the administration to easily connect with the student body. The app contains a calendar, alert page, episode list for our school's news program, and a page dedicated to student events. The app's content delivery backend is done in Node.js, and uses other systems to update the content of the app. This is used to update the calendar, alerts, and ASB tabs of the app.
After my graduation, I passed down the app to the next generation of App Developers, who continue to maintian and update the app.
